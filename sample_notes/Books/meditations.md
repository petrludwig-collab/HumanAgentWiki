**Author:** [[Marcus Aurelius]] · **Written:** c. 161–180 AD · **Signature quote:** [[Waste no more time — be one]]

The private notebook of a Roman emperor, never meant for publication. Written as Stoic
self-reminders, it returns again and again to one discipline: focus on **what is in your control**
— your own judgments and actions — and let go of the rest.

## Why it matters
- The foundational text of practical **Stoicism**.
- Calm, blunt advice on duty, mortality, anger, and attention — still startlingly modern.
- Reads like advice from a wise friend across two millennia.
