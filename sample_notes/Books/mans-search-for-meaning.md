**Author:** [[Viktor E. Frankl]] · **Published:** 1946 (German), 1959 (English) · **Signature quote:** [[The last of the human freedoms]]

Part memoir of Frankl's survival in Nazi concentration camps, part introduction to
**logotherapy** — his school of psychology built on the idea that the primary human drive is the
search for meaning. Even in the worst suffering, he argues, we keep the freedom to choose our response.

## Why it matters
- One of the most influential books on **resilience and purpose** ever written.
- Logotherapy: meaning can be found in work, in love, and in the attitude we take toward unavoidable suffering.
- Short, humane, and unforgettable.
