This is your **second brain** — plain Markdown notes that link to each other and become a
searchable, visual knowledge graph. This starter pack shows how it fits together; replace it
with your own notes whenever you like.

## How it works
- **One note = one idea.** Keep notes small and link them generously.
- **Link notes by wrapping a title in double square brackets** — for example [[Atomic Habits]] —
  and the link becomes an edge in the graph.
- **Categories** come from the top folder (or a `category:` field). This demo has three:
  [[Books]], [[Authors]], [[Quotes]].
- **Search is hybrid** (meaning + keywords). Ask in plain language and it finds by sense, not just words.
- **Your AI agents** can read everything through the MCP tools `brain_search`, `brain_get`,
  and `brain_neighbors`.

## What's in this demo
- **[[Books]]** — five books worth reading.
- **[[Authors]]** — the people who wrote them.
- **[[Quotes]]** — one memorable line from each book.

Follow a link, then follow another — that's the whole idea. A good place to start:
[[Man's Search for Meaning]] or [[Meditations]].
