**Wrote:** [[Meditations]] · **121–180 AD**

Roman emperor from 161 to 180 AD and a practicing **Stoic philosopher** — often counted among the
"Five Good Emperors." He ruled an empire while writing private notes to himself on how to live
with virtue, patience, and reason.

Those notes, never intended for others, became [[Meditations]] — one of history's most enduring
works of practical philosophy.
