**Wrote:** [[Thinking, Fast and Slow]] · **1934–2024**

Israeli-American psychologist whose work with Amos Tversky founded the field of
**behavioral economics**. He won the **2002 Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences** for
showing that human decisions systematically depart from the "rational actor" model.

His big idea: judgment runs on two systems — a fast intuitive one and a slow deliberate one —
and the fast one is riddled with predictable biases.
